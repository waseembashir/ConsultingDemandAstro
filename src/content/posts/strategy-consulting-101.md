Strategy consulting has a mystique problem. The tier-one firms have built an industry out of selling frameworks with names that sound profound until you realise they're just structured common sense.

This isn't a takedown of the discipline — good strategic advice is genuinely valuable. It's a guide to understanding what you're buying so you can evaluate whether the price is justified.

## What Strategy Consulting Is (and Isn't)

Strategy consulting, at its best, is structured outside-in thinking. The consultant brings three things you can't easily generate internally:

1. **Pattern recognition across industries** — they've seen this exact situation before, in a different sector
2. **Permission to ask uncomfortable questions** — they have no internal politics to navigate
3. **Structured synthesis** — the ability to turn messy inputs into clear choices

What it isn't: a substitute for your own judgement. The best strategy engagements end with you making clearer decisions, not with the consultant making decisions for you.

## The Five Types of Strategy Engagement

| Type | When to Use |
|---|---|
| Corporate strategy | Setting 3–5 year direction and resource allocation |
| Growth strategy | Identifying new markets, products, or channels |
| Competitive positioning | Understanding where you win and lose |
| Go-to-market strategy | Launching into a new segment or geography |
| Portfolio strategy | Deciding which business units to build, buy, or sell |

## What Good Looks Like

A quality strategy engagement delivers a **decision memo**: a clear document that frames the two or three most important choices facing the business, the evidence for each option, and a recommended course of action with rationale.

It should not be a 100-slide deck of market data. If your consultant's primary output is a deck of external research you could have bought from Statista, the engagement was poorly scoped.

## The Right Time to Hire

Strategy consultants are most valuable at decision inflection points:

- Before a funding round, when you need to stress-test your growth thesis
- When a market shift makes your current strategy uncertain
- When internal disagreement is blocking progress and you need external arbitration
- When a competitor has made an unexpected move and you need rapid scenario planning

## Pricing Reality

Senior strategy consultants (independent) charge £1,500–£3,000/day in the UK. Tier-one firms (McKinsey, Bain, BCG) charge significantly more — and you're paying a premium for the brand as much as the thinking.

For most businesses under £50M revenue, an independent strategy consultant or a boutique advisory firm will outperform a tier-one firm on value-for-money, because you get the partner doing the work rather than a junior analyst.
